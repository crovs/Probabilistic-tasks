What To Understand

This list is about choosing the correct model before calculating. The important part is not only the number, but why the problem is a permutation, combination, k-permutation, or sequence with repetition.

Best Visuals

Use a line of positions for permutations, a round table for circular permutations, a podium for ordered selections, code boxes for repeated sequences, and grouped objects for combinations.

What To Say Aloud

Start with: "The outcome here is ...". Then say whether order matters, whether repetition is allowed, and whether all objects or only some are used. That sentence should determine the model.
