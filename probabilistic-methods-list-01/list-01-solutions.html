What To Understand

Do not stop at the formula. For this list, the key ideas are: what an elementary outcome means, why order matters, and why Buffon's needle is continuous instead of discrete.

Best Visuals

Use a tree for coin and die experiments, boxes for card replacement vs no replacement, a 7-position sequence for weather, and a shaded rectangle in the (x, theta) plane for Buffon's needle.

What To Say Aloud

Start with: "One elementary outcome here is ...". Then say whether order matters, define the event as a subset of the sample space, and only after that compute the probability.
